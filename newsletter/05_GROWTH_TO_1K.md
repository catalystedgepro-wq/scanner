1. **Reddit — r/pennystocks, r/smallstreetbets, r/stocks, r/wallstreetbetsOG.**
   Why: catalyst/SEC-filing content lands there natively. Most posts are unsourced — an audited scoreboard is a contrarian shock format. No self-promo rule violation if posts lead with data, link to Beehiiv only in author bio / one pinned comment.
   Cadence: 3 posts/week, rotating subs. Every post = one scoreboard screenshot + one filing-level insight. Never just "subscribe to my newsletter."

2. **X/Twitter — quote-replying to catalyst/SEC-filing accounts in real time.**
   Why: catalyst Twitter is concentrated among ~30 accounts (Benzinga, Deltaone, StockTwits bots, unusual-whales, SEC filing alert bots). Every 8-K they post is an opportunity to reply with the model's probability + historical base rate for that filing type — instant authority, zero ad spend.
   Cadence: 10 quote-replies/day, auto-queued. Profile header = Beehiiv subscribe link.

3. **SEO — long-tail EDGAR filing questions.**
   Why: "what is an S-3 shelf offering," "how to read an 8-K," "424B5 dilution meaning" — each gets thousands of searches/month, low-difficulty KD, and the reader intent is *exactly* the target buyer. Free traffic compounds past 90 days.
   Cadence: 2 SEO articles/week on catalystedge.beehiiv.com and/or a microsite. Each article embeds a sample issue + scoreboard widget.

## Lead Magnet (converts visitor → subscriber in <10 min)

**"The 7-Filing Cheat Sheet: every SEC form a small-cap trader actually needs to know, with the historical next-session hit rate."**

Format: one-page PDF + one-screen interactive table. Shows:
- 8-K item numbers that move stocks (1.01, 2.01, 5.02, 8.01) and the ones that don't
- S-3 vs 424B vs 424B5 dilution difference in plain English
- Form 4 insider buys vs insider sells (which one actually predicts moves)
- Each row includes base rate for +2% / +5% next session from the 8,445-row outcome dataset

Consumable in under 10 minutes. Solves one precise pain (filing alphabet soup). Gated by email. Lives at `catalystedge.beehiiv.com/cheat-sheet`.

Not a 40-page ebook. Not a video course. One page, printable, useful tomorrow morning.

## Cross-Promotion Strategy (complementary, not competing)

Targets = newsletters whose audience trades but does NOT focus on SEC-filing catalysts:
- **Technical analysis newsletters** — their readers need fundamental triggers; we provide them. Pitch: "your readers get the chart setup; my readers get the catalyst. Let's swap one issue."
- **Macro / options-flow newsletters** — macro guys want micro triggers to deploy thematic trades; options-flow guys want filing context for unusual volume. Complementary.
- **Investing podcasts with small-cap episodes** — offer a monthly "state of the catalyst tape" segment in exchange for show-notes link.

Explicit exclusions (direct competitors, no swap):
- Any catalyst-specific newsletter (Trade Ideas, Benzinga Pro writeups, Stocktwits alerts).
- Any penny-stock pump service — reputational poison.

Swap mechanic: dedicated issue exchange, not a "check this out" footer. 1 swap / week for 13 weeks = 13 partner-audience exposures.
