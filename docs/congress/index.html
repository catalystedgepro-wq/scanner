Why Track Congressional Trades?

Members of Congress have access to non-public briefings, regulatory previews, and policy decisions that move markets. The STOCK Act requires them to disclose trades within 45 days, but the information edge can still be significant.

  • Timing advantage — Congress often trades ahead of policy announcements
  • Committee insight — Members on banking, energy, or defense committees trade in their sectors
  • Cluster signals — Multiple members buying the same stock is a high-conviction indicator
  • Bipartisan alignment — When both parties buy the same stock, the signal strengthens

Congress + SEC Catalysts

The most powerful trades happen when congressional activity aligns with SEC filing catalysts:

  • Congress buys + 8-K material event = institutional front-running confirmation
  • Senator sells + S-3 dilution filing = smart money exit before retail
  • Committee member buys + Form 4 insider cluster = triple-validated conviction
