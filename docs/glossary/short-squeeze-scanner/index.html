What Makes a Squeeze Setup?

Three conditions must align for a squeeze to trigger:

  1. High short interest — 15%+ of float held short means significant bearish positioning
  2. Low days-to-cover (DTC) — When DTC compresses, shorts have less room to exit gracefully
  3. Catalyst trigger — A positive SEC filing, earnings beat, or news event forces covering
The catalyst is the key. Many stocks sit at high short interest for months without squeezing. It's the SEC filing catalyst — an 8-K material event, a Form 4 insider buy cluster, or a 13D activist stake — that lights the fuse. Our scanner scores both squeeze pressure AND catalyst quality.

How to Use the Scanner for Squeeze Plays

  1. Open the Catalyst Edge Scanner
  2. Sort by squeeze score (descending) to find highest pressure setups
  3. Filter for gap score > 14 to ensure a catalyst is present
  4. Check the filing type — 8-K and Form 4 are the strongest squeeze triggers
  5. Verify the stock has adequate volume (avg > 500K) for clean entries

Common Squeeze Traps to Avoid

  • High SI alone isn't enough — Without a catalyst, shorts can hold indefinitely
  • Watch for shelf registrations — An S-3 filing kills squeeze setups by creating dilution overhang
  • Low float traps — Micro-caps with tiny floats can spike and reverse within minutes
  • Late entries — If the squeeze already ran 50%+, the risk/reward has flipped
