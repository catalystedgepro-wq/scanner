Why SEC filings matter more during earnings

Every quarter, roughly 4,000 companies report earnings within a 6-week window. Most traders watch analyst estimates and options implied moves. Few look at what insiders were doing in the weeks before the report. SEC filings filed during the "quiet period" (typically 2-4 weeks before earnings) contain signals that the market hasn't priced in yet.

Companies can't talk publicly during the quiet period, but insiders can still buy and sell stock. Form 4 filings during this window are among the strongest earnings predictors available to retail traders.

SEC filing signals before earnings

Bullish

Insider Buying (Form 4)

CEO or CFO open-market purchases 2-6 weeks before earnings. They know the numbers. Studies show pre-earnings insider buys precede beats 70%+ of the time.

Bullish

Positive 8-K Catalysts

Contract wins, FDA approvals, or partnership announcements filed during the quiet period. These events will likely boost the upcoming quarterly results.

Bearish

S-3 Shelf Registration

Filing a shelf offering right before earnings often means management expects weak results and is preparing to raise cash through dilution.

Bearish

Insider Selling Clusters

Multiple insiders filing Form 4 sells in the weeks before earnings. Especially bearish when the selling is from C-suite officers, not just directors.

Bullish

13D Accumulation

An activist investor crossing the 5% threshold (Schedule 13D) before earnings signals they expect a catalyst event.

Bullish

Unusual Options Flow

Large call sweeps or block trades in the days before earnings, especially at strike prices well above current price, suggest informed bullish positioning.

Pre-earnings watchlist workflow

4 weeks before earnings

Scan for Form 4 insider purchases at companies reporting in the upcoming season. Build initial watchlist from cluster buys and C-suite purchases.

2 weeks before earnings

Cross-reference insider buying with Catalyst Score. Filter for stocks scoring 10+ with recent insider activity. Check for recent positive 8-K filings.

1 week before earnings

Layer in options flow data. Look for unusual call buying or bullish sweeps that confirm the insider signal. Check dark pool prints for institutional accumulation.

Day before earnings

Finalize position sizing. Review implied move from options market. Set stop losses. Know the reporting time (before open vs after close).

Post-earnings

Read the 8-K earnings release and 10-Q. Watch for sympathy plays in the same sector. Strong beats often lift the entire industry group.

Common earnings season mistakes

  • Ignoring the quiet period: Filings during this window are more significant because management can't use PR to move the stock.
  • Only watching one signal: Insider buying alone is good. Insider buying + unusual options flow + high catalyst score = much better. Convergence is the edge.
  • Holding through earnings without a thesis: Unless SEC filings confirm directional conviction, earnings plays are coin flips with elevated IV premiums working against you.
  • Missing the post-earnings window: The best trades often happen 2-5 days after earnings as the market digests the report and SEC filings. The initial gap isn't always the final move.
  • Overlooking the 10-K/10-Q: The actual 10-K filing follows the earnings press release by days or weeks. Risk factor changes and management discussion shifts create secondary catalysts.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is earnings season?

Earnings season happens four times a year, starting about two weeks after each quarter ends. The busiest weeks are typically mid-January through mid-February (Q4), mid-April through mid-May (Q1), mid-July through mid-August (Q2), and mid-October through mid-November (Q3). About 70% of S&P 500 companies report within a 3-week window each season.

Which SEC filings predict earnings surprises?

Form 4 insider buying in the 30 days before earnings is the strongest predictor. If a CEO buys shares weeks before reporting, they likely know the numbers are strong. 8-K filings about contract wins, product launches, or partnership announcements filed during the quiet period also signal positive results. Conversely, S-3 shelf registrations filed right before earnings often precede weak results followed by dilution.

How do you trade earnings with SEC filing data?

Build a pre-earnings watchlist by scanning for companies with recent insider buying (Form 4), positive 8-K catalysts, and high catalyst scores. Avoid stocks with recent S-3 registrations or insider selling. Use options flow data to confirm institutional positioning, and look for convergence between insider buying and unusual call activity. This approach identifies stocks most likely to beat expectations.
