What Are Dark Pools?

Dark pools are private exchanges where institutional investors execute large block trades without showing their orders on public exchanges. This prevents large orders from moving the market before they're filled.

Examples include Crossfinder (Credit Suisse), SIGMA X (Goldman Sachs), and MS Pool (Morgan Stanley). FINRA publishes aggregate dark pool volume data with a delay.

Why Dark Pool Volume Matters

When dark pool volume for a ticker spikes relative to its average, it signals institutional interest. This is particularly powerful when combined with other catalyst signals:

Reading Dark Pool Data

Volume Ratio Interpretation

  • 1.0x baseline — Normal institutional flow
  • 1.5-2.0x — Elevated interest, worth monitoring
  • 2.0-3.0x — Significant accumulation/distribution signal
  • >3.0x — Extreme institutional activity, often precedes major move

Timing Matters

Dark pool prints that occur in the final hour of trading or post-market carry more signal weight — institutions often try to fill large orders at close to minimize market impact.
